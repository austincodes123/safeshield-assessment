2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by Safeshield, including but not limited to:

  • Compensation structures, commission splits, bonus programs, and incentive plans
  • Business strategies, financial data, pricing models, and operational methods
  • Client lists, lead sources, referral relationships, and account data
  • Proprietary technology, software systems, workflows, scripts, and processes
  • Training materials, playbooks, and internal documentation
  • Marketing strategies, advertising spend, and campaign data
  • Employee and contractor information

3. Obligations of Recipient

Recipient agrees to:

  • Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
  • Not disclose, share, publish, or distribute any Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent from Safeshield
  • Not use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than evaluating and/or participating in a business relationship with Safeshield
  • Not photograph, screenshot, copy, or reproduce any Confidential Information by any means
  • Take all reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of the information

4. Non-Solicitation

During the term of any business relationship with Safeshield and for twenty-four (24) months following its conclusion, Recipient shall not directly or indirectly:

  • Solicit or service any Safeshield client, lead, or referral partner
  • Solicit or hire any Safeshield employee or contractor
  • Circumvent Safeshield to conduct insurance business with any of the Company's relationships

5. Return of Materials

Upon request by Safeshield or upon termination of any business relationship, Recipient shall immediately return or destroy all Confidential Information in their possession, including any copies, notes, summaries, or derivatives.

6. Remedies

Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Safeshield for which monetary damages may be insufficient. Safeshield shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

7. Term

The obligations of confidentiality and non-disclosure under this Agreement shall survive for a period of five (5) years from the date of disclosure of any Confidential Information, regardless of whether a business relationship is established.

8. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Connecticut. Any dispute arising from this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration seated in Connecticut.

What We Expect

Safeshield producers are held to a standard. These aren't suggestions. This is how we operate.

Your Phone

Your phone is your most important tool. Clients call, you answer. If you can't answer, you call back within 30 minutes. No exceptions. A missed call is a missed opportunity. A missed renewal call is money out of both our pockets. Your phone should be on, charged, and within reach during all business hours.

Your Email

Every client email gets a response within 2 hours during business days. Even if the answer is "I'm looking into this and will get back to you by end of day." Silence kills trust. Trust kills retention. Retention kills your income.

Renewals Are Everything

This is where your wealth is built. Every policy you write renews 12 months later. If that client stays, you get paid again without lifting a finger. If they leave because you didn't pick up the phone, that's money you'll never get back.

You are responsible for keeping your clients happy and retained. That means proactive outreach 60 days before expiration. Know when your clients' policies are up. Be the reason they stay, not the reason they leave.

Daily Activity

Production doesn't happen by accident. You should be quoting every single day. Following up on every lead the same day it comes in. Tracking your pipeline. If you're not quoting, you're not earning. If you're not following up, someone else is. This business rewards the relentless.

Represent the Brand

When you pick up the phone, you are Safeshield. When you send an email, you are Safeshield. When you meet a client, you are Safeshield. Every interaction reflects everything we've built. Professionalism is the minimum, not the ceiling.

Use the Systems

Every quote, every bind, every client interaction gets logged in NowCerts. No exceptions. If it's not in the system, it didn't happen. Use CASEY for workflow management. The tools exist to make you faster so you can focus on what pays: selling and servicing.

The Long Game

This is the most important section of this entire document. Read it twice.

Insurance is not a get-rich-quick business. It is a get-rich-for-certain business. The producers who build wealth in this industry understand one concept: compounding renewals.

Every policy you write today pays you again next year. And the year after. Your book of business is an asset that grows every single year as long as you keep your clients happy and keep writing new business on top. The math is simple. The discipline is hard. But the result is inevitable.

"Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it. He who doesn't, pays it."
- Albert Einstein
Here's What Patience Looks Like

Think about it this way. Every policy you write today pays you commission. When it renews next year, you get paid again. And the year after that. And the year after that. Meanwhile you're still writing new business on top of those renewals.

Year one, you're building the foundation. Year two, the renewals start stacking. Year three, the stack gets bigger. By year five, your renewal income alone starts to become serious money. By year ten, producers who stayed the course in this industry are earning multiples of what they made in year one.

The math is simple and it is inevitable. The only variable is how hard you work and how long you stay. The producers making $200K, $300K, $500K per year didn't get there by switching careers every two years. They got there by grinding through the early years and letting the renewals compound.

The longer you do this, the more you make. Every year is better than the last. There is no ceiling. There is no cap.

"The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient."
- Warren Buffett
Year One Is the Hardest

Every successful producer went through it. Every agency owner went through it. The ones who made it through year one and kept grinding are the ones earning $200K, $300K, $500K per year today.

Be patient. Be consistent. Trust the process. The math works. It always works. You just have to stay long enough to see it.
